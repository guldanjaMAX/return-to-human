The Story

Every wound was a doorway. Every loss was an initiation.

1996

The Loss That Started Everything

Anthony was nine years old when his father died. He was forty two. A man full of life and love, gone before his time. That single event cracked open a boy's world and planted a question that would take decades to answer: What does it mean to truly live before you die? Anthony watched his mother fight to hold the family together. He watched himself decide, somewhere deep in his bones, that he would never be unprepared. That he would build a body and a life strong enough to withstand anything.

2000s

The Building

The pain became fuel. At fifteen, Anthony was already in the gym, transforming grief into muscle and discipline into armor. He became a competitive bodybuilder. He went on to earn his doctorate in naturopathic medicine. He channeled everything he had learned about losing his father too young into a mission to help other families never feel that loss. He founded the Fit Father Project and the Fit Mother Project, building them into multi million dollar health companies that have served tens of thousands of families around the world. From the outside, it looked like the ultimate success story. On the inside, the wound that drove it all was still running.

2015

The Breaking

On a ski slope in the mountains, Anthony ignored an intuition to slow down. He hit a tree at full speed. Shattered leg. Broken arm. Nearly died. That accident set off a decade of reconstruction: seven surgeries, a leg lengthening procedure, and the slow unraveling of the ego that had been built to outrun the grief of a nine year old boy. The man who had spent his whole life building control was forced to sit with powerlessness. And in that stillness, something cracked open that no amount of muscle could close.

2015 to 2020

The Descent

Through ten years of recovery, Anthony learned something the gym never taught him: how to sit with pain instead of running from it. He discovered meditation. He learned forgiveness. He began to see the energy running his personality for what it was, a survival mechanism born from grief. The man who had built everything to feel safe began to let it all go. The armor softened. The questions deepened. And for the first time, Anthony began to search not for how to live longer, but for what it actually means to be alive.

The Amazon

The Fire

Anthony traveled to the Amazon. He sat with plant medicine. He spent a full week in total darkness. And there, in the silence, in the void, he found God. Not the God he was taught about as a child. A direct, personal, felt communion with Creator. Everything made sense. Every loss, every breaking, every surgery, every night of doubt. It was all preparation. The through line became clear: every wound was a doorway. Every loss was an initiation. Every surrender built the man who could now hold space for others walking through their own fire.

Fatherhood

The Return

Anthony became a father. His daughter Brooke arrived and his heart broke open again in the most beautiful way. He understood, for the first time, the depth of his own father's love. He understood the sacrifice. He understood what it means to hold someone so small and know you would give everything for them. Then his marriage ended, and he surrendered into that too. Every opening, every breaking, every moment of raw vulnerability brought him closer to what he now calls center.

Now

The Center

Today, Anthony lives in Sedona, Arizona. He continues to lead the Fit Father Project and Fit Mother Project, serving families around the world. And alongside that work, he has stepped into something sacred. He works one on one with men who are ready to wake up. Through physical alignment, spiritual ceremony, time in the wild, and deep relational work, he helps accomplished men reconnect to their bodies, find their own direct relationship with God, face their deepest fears, and align their entire lives around their sacred center. He plays the Native American flute by the fire while they journey inward. He holds the space. And then he sends them home. Transformed, alive, and free.
